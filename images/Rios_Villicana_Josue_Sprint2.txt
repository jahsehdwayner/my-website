Persona 1 – Tammy (Busy Student Buyer) Age: 20 Major: Business Year:
Sophomore

Interview Source: Interviewed UW Bothell student (April 2026)

Bio: Tammy is a busy college student who often eats between classes. She
prefers quick and affordable meals and doesn’t like wasting time waiting
in long lines.

Goals: - Get food quickly - Stay within budget - Easy ordering

Frustrations: - Long wait times - Doesn’t know truck locations -
Confusing menus

Needs: - Clear menu display - Ability to order ahead - Location tracking

Quote: “I hate waiting in long lines between classes.”

Delighter: - Mobile order + fast pickup

Persona 2 – Aidan (Efficiency-Focused Student) Age: 22 Major: Computer
Science Year: Senior

Interview Source: Interviewed UW Bothell student (April 2026)

Bio: Aidan values efficiency and convenience. He often eats with friends
and prefers using technology to make everything faster and easier.

Goals: - Fast service - High-quality food - Easy payment

Frustrations: - Slow service - No online ordering - Hard to split
payments

Needs: - Mobile ordering system - Digital payments - Order tracking

Quote: “I wish I could just order ahead and pick it up.”

Delighter: - Real-time order updates

Persona 3 – Krissy (Social Experience Student) Age: 19 Major: Psychology
Year: Freshman

Interview Source: Interviewed UW Bothell student (April 2026)

Bio: Krissy sees food trucks as a social experience. She enjoys trying
new foods with friends and values variety and recommendations.

Goals: - Try new foods - Have fun with friends - Find good deals

Frustrations: - Hard to decide what to order - No reviews or ratings -
Limited variety

Needs: - Food photos - Reviews/ratings - Deals or discounts

Quote: “I usually don’t know what to order, I just guess.”

Delighter: - Trending items + student discounts

Reflection

To create my personas, I focused on common student behaviors such as
convenience, cost, and social experience. I used simple interview-style
questions to understand what students want, what frustrates them, and
what would improve their experience. I determined the personas were
“good enough” when each one clearly represented a different type of user
with unique goals and pain points.

The persona I am most proud of is Aidan because it clearly connects
technology with user needs. His focus on efficiency and digital tools
directly relates to how software solutions can improve the food truck
experience.

The most useful persona for the team would likely be Tammy. She
represents a large group of customers who need fast and affordable food,
making her highly relevant to business success. By solving her problems,
such as long wait times and unclear information, the company can improve
overall customer satisfaction and efficiency.
